# Product Thesis

## The Problem

In regulated perishable trade (mango export from Peru/Mexico), buyers, financiers, and compliance teams cannot make timely decisions because evidence is:

- Scattered across emails, PDFs, WhatsApp messages
- Unattributed (who said what, when, with what proof)
- Incomplete (missing certificates, expired docs, custody gaps)
- Not machine-readable or verifiable

## The Product

A Consignment Readiness Engine that:

1. Groups lots into consignment cases (the operational unit)
2. Attaches first-class evidence objects (typed, hashed, sourced)
3. Records attributed attestations (who claims what, backed by what evidence)
4. Detects exceptions automatically (blocking/warning decision blockers)
5. Computes state snapshots (completeness, attribution, custody, readiness)
6. Generates verifiable evidence packs (exportable, hashable, shareable)
7. Anchors hashes on-chain (without user wallet friction)

## The Wedge

Not "blockchain traceability." Not "QR scan."

The wedge is: **reducing critical uncertainty for trade decisions.**

A buyer or financier can look at a consignment and know, computably:
- Is the evidence complete?
- Are claims attributed?
- Is custody continuous?
- Are there blockers?
- Can I make a decision?

## Two Views, One Engine

- **Import/Compliance Readiness**: blocking exceptions, evidence completeness, attestation coverage, custody continuity
- **Financing/Underwriting Readiness**: evidence sufficiency, custody score, exception count, doc recency, eligibility

Same snapshot. Different lens. Not two products.
